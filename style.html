Criminals and their crimes

Giuseppe Antonio Doto

Joe Adonis (born Giuseppe Antonio Doto; November 22, 1902 - November 26, 1971) was an Italian-American mobster and influential crime boss. Known for his charm and sharp appearance, he was a key figure in organized crime during the early to mid-20th century, working closely with Charles "Lucky" Luciano to shape the modern American Mafia.

Crimes :

Adonis profited heavily during Prohibition by smuggling and distributing alcohol. He operated numerous illegal gambling establishments and was involved in extortion and loan sharking. He manipulated labor unions to extort businesses and increase Mafia control.

Adolf Hitler

Adolf Hitler (April 20, 1889 - April 30, 1945) was a German politician and leader of the Nazi Party (National Socialist German Workers' Party). He rose to power as Chancellor of Germany in 1933 and later assumed the title of Führer (Leader) in 1934, establishing a totalitarian regime. Hitler's ideology and policies led to World War II and the Holocaust, making him one of history's most infamous figures

Crimes :

Hitler's aggressive expansionist policies and invasion of Poland in 1939 triggered World War II, leading to widespread destruction and the deaths of an estimated 70-85 million people globally.Hitler orchestrated the systematic genocide of 6 million Jews, along with millions of others, including Romani people, disabled individuals, Poles, Soviet prisoners of war, and other persecuted groups.

Pablo Escobar

Pablo Escobar (1949-1993) was a Colombian drug lord and the founder of the Medellín Cartel, which became the most powerful and violent drug trafficking organization in history. Escobar controlled over 80% of the global cocaine trade during the 1980s, earning billions of dollars annually.

Crimes :

Smuggled massive quantities of cocaine into the United States and other countries, generating immense wealth.Ordered the assassination of thousands, including judges, politicians, journalists, and police officers. Orchestrated bombings, such as the 1989 destruction of Avianca Flight 203, killing 110 people.
